## What It Is

A compact prompt for summarizing a chapter into key points, supporting subpoints, relationships between ideas, and the main messages worth remembering.

## When To Use

Use this when reading a chapter, paper section, or long-form document and you want a repeatable summary format that captures both structure and takeaway value.

## Key Details

- Asks for the chapter's key points rather than a loose free-form summary.
- Requires support for each key point through its subpoints and evidence or rationale.
- Prompts for relationships across the main points so the final summary captures the overall structure of the argument.
- Ends by asking what message or key points the author wants the reader to remember.
- Works well as a reusable scaffold for reading notes that may later be ingested into the wiki.
